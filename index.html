Questions

The six things you'll ask before signing up.

Is BlueLetter affiliated with the Dutch government? +
No. BlueLetter is an independent translation service built in Amsterdam. The official government channels remain Mijn Overheid, Mijn Belastingdienst, and each sender's own portal. We don't replace them — we just make their letters readable.
Which letters does it support? +
Letters from the seven senders that account for about 95% of international mail: Belastingdienst, DUO, IND, CJIB, Gemeente, UWV, and KvK. Other senders work too — they just don't get a custom icon yet.
Can I trust the translation for legal decisions? +
For day-to-day understanding, yes. For high-stakes decisions — residence-permit renewals, large fines, court correspondence — verify with the original Dutch text or a qualified translator. Every BlueLetter translation carries this disclaimer in the app.
How much will it cost? +
Two free scans per month, forever. Unlimited Pro is €4.99/month or €39/year. Early-access signups get the year price locked at €29 for life — that's the only launch promise we're making.
What languages do you translate into? +
English at launch. Spanish, Polish, Hindi, Mandarin, Turkish, and Arabic are queued for Q3 2026 — in the order of the largest international communities in the Netherlands. Vote for yours when you sign up.
When does it actually launch? +
Public iOS launch: September 2026. Closed TestFlight beta: July 2026 (first 500 on the waitlist). Android: 2027 if iOS proves the model. We'll email you the moment your slot opens.
A note from the founder

I built BlueLetter after my third missed deadline.

In 2023, a Belastingdienst envelope sat unopened on my counter for six weeks. By the time I dragged it through Google Translate, the deadline was four days gone. The fine was €260. I'd lived in the Netherlands for two years.

So I asked around. Every international I knew had the same story — a DUO letter no one could read, an IND form bounced because of one missed checkbox, a Gemeente notice that turned out to be a parking ticket eight months later. We were all collectively losing thousands of euros to envelopes.

The Dutch government is excellent. The letters aren't the problem. The problem is they only arrive in Dutch, on paper, and assume you can read both. For 1.3 million internationals here, that assumption costs us money — and sometimes status.

BlueLetter reads every letter for you. Scan it, get the translation, get the deadline. That's the whole product. If you've ever stood in your hallway holding an envelope you couldn't read, this is for you.
