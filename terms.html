1. What BlueLetter is

BlueLetter is a mobile translation aid. You take a photo of a Dutch government letter using your device camera or upload an existing image; BlueLetter sends the image to a large-language-model AI provider, and returns:

  • a translation of the letter into a language you choose;
  • a plain-language summary of the letter's content;
  • a list of dates, deadlines, and amounts the AI identified;
  • optionally, the ability to add the deadlines to your device calendar and set local push reminders (paid tiers only).

BlueLetter is a convenience tool. It is not an official translation service, a notarised translation, a sworn translator, a tax advisor, an immigration advisor, a lawyer, an accountant, a financial advisor, a debt-collection agent, or a government authority.

6. AI translation — scope and limitations

BlueLetter uses third-party large-language-model AI to read and interpret the images you upload. The model used at the time of writing is Anthropic Claude, accessed through the Anthropic API. The model used may change over time without notice to you.

AI output is probabilistic and can be wrong. The translation, summary, and extracted deadlines that BlueLetter returns may be:
  • incomplete (omitting important information);
  • incorrect (mistranslating, misclassifying, or hallucinating content);
  • partially correct but misleading in context;
  • outdated (a translation may not reflect later government correspondence, changes to your file, or updates to Dutch law);
  • unable to read poor-quality, partially-cropped, glare-affected, or handwritten content.
You must not rely on BlueLetter as the sole basis for any decision with financial, legal, immigration, residence, tax, employment, healthcare, or other significant consequences.
